Communication & Multimedia Design

I study Communication & Multimedia Design (CMD) at Zuyd Hogeschool Maastricht. Here I study visual design, UX-design, animation and much more. I also study coding and programming, a skill I use often. This website is an example of that. A good example of a project where a lot of my skills came together, is the video playing above. This is a project where I made, and programmed, randomly generated art to accompany a live jazz band. Below are a few more projects I've done as a CMD student.

Code4Kids

At the end of my first year at CMD, we did a project where we had to supply a workshop for grade school students to teach them basic coding. That's where Codingcat was born. I was mostly responsible for the visual design on the project. I designed the logo, the website, a boardgame, and much more. We also gave the workshop to a full class of 12-year-olds, which was a lot of fun. The website we made is still up and running at coding-cat.nl

Multimedia Story

Halfway through my second year at CMD, we did a project for the Centraal Bureau voor Statistieken (CBS). My team was asked to make a story explaining the relation between soy from Brazil and meat in China. I was mainly responsible for the illustrations and animations on the project. Follow this link to view the story yourself.

2D Animation

In my second year, I followed an elective where I learned to make 2D animations. I experimented with a lot of different animation styles and I made a short animation about a girl and a dragon who live by a waterfall. You can watch it here.
